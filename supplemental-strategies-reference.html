Supplemental Tax Strategies — Reference

Per-dollar figures are what the RETT engine actually computes. "Offset / $1" = deduction produced per dollar deployed; "Saved / $1" assumes a ~40.5% top ordinary rate (37% fed + 3.5% KY) and ~23.8% long-term.

Read this first: of the twelve, only Delphi and appreciated-asset Charitable gifts reach the capital gain from the sale. The other ten reduce ordinary income (W-2, rental, §1250 recapture) — they run alongside the gain, they don't shrink the LTCG. The three physical-asset plays (Heavy Vehicle, Aircraft, Equipment) only make sense when the client wanted the asset anyway: saving ~40¢ on the dollar still costs ~60¢ net.

Oil & Gas Working Interest

KEEP
Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §263(c) + Reg. §1.612-4 (expense IDC now); §469(c)(3) (working interest is active, not passive); §613A depletion later.
MethodYear-1 Intangible Drilling Cost deduction = a fraction of capital invested, expensed in year 0/1.
Per dollar75¢–95¢ offset (engine default 95%) → ~30–38¢ tax saved.
Applies: client has ordinary income to shelter + tolerance for illiquid, real-risk direct investment.
Skip: no ordinary income (becomes NOL) or AMT-bound — excess IDC is an AMT preference; engine warns the Y1 figure overstates.
2

Delphi Fund

KEEP
Ordinary → Capital
AuthorityThinnest of the set — partnership character pass-through §702(b)/§704(b), §1211/§1212, §901 FTC. No single section blesses it.
MethodPer $1 net invested, the K-1 throws −30% ordinary expense, +25% LT gain, +6% qual div, −5% ST loss, −1% FTC.
Per dollar~30¢ gross ordinary offset, but net ~8¢ after LT tax on the gain handed back.
Applies: big ordinary income AND big LT gain to absorb the +25%; meets min ($1M Class B / $5M Class A) and accepts lockup.
Skip: small account, no ordinary income, or low appetite for an aggressive structured fund.
3

PTET — Pass-Through Entity Tax

Ordinary
AuthorityIRS Notice 2020-75 + state PTET statutes; sidesteps the §164 $10K SALT cap.
MethodEntity pays your state income tax and deducts it federally; you take a state credit.
Per dollar of PTET paid~$1 federal deduction → ~30¢ saved (20% QBI haircut applied).
Applies: client owns a pass-through in a state with a PTET regime (KY = 4.0%, in the engine's map).
Skip: no pass-through income, or a no-income-tax state (TX, FL, WY, NV, WA, etc.).
4

Charitable Gifts

Ordinary + Capital
AuthorityIRC §170 (60% AGI cash / 30% appreciated; deduct at fair market value).
MethodGift cash or appreciated stock; deduct FMV and avoid the capital-gains tax on the built-in gain.
Per dollar giftedCash → ~40¢ saved (dollar is gone). Appreciated → ~40¢ deduction + ~24¢ avoided LT tax.
Applies: client is genuinely charitable — best with low-basis appreciated stock.
Skip: not charitable (never a net profit), or already over AGI caps (excess carries 5 years).
5

Cost Segregation

Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §168(k) bonus + §167; Hospital Corp. of America v. Comm'r, 109 T.C. 21.
MethodReclassify 20–35% of building basis into 5/7/15-yr property; take it as year-1 bonus depreciation.
Per dollar of depreciable basis~27¢ (apartment) to ~37¢ (industrial) year-1 deduction → ~11–15¢ saved.
Applies: client owns or buys depreciable real estate (best newly acquired this year).
Skip: no real estate, land-heavy property, or a near-term resale (recapture claws it back).
6

Heavy Vehicle Deduction

Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §179 + §168(k) bonus; §280F luxury caps.
Method>6,000-lb GVWR business vehicle — §179 (heavy SUV capped $32K) + 100% bonus on the residual.
Per dollar of vehicle cost~$1 offset year 1 (× business-use %).
Applies: needs a heavy vehicle with >50% business use.
Skip: ≤50% business use (drops to ADS straight-line, §280F(b)(1)) or a light passenger auto (luxury cap ~$20,300 guts it).
7

Equipment Leasing Fund

Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §168(k) bonus; §469 + Reg. §1.469-5T material participation.
MethodInvest in a fund that buys equipment; year-1 K-1 bonus loss ≈ 90% of capital.
Per dollar~90¢ offset → ~36¢ saved.
Applies: client will commit ≥100 hrs/yr so the loss is non-passive.
Skip: passive investor (loss suspends — zero current benefit). High yield, high audit surface; §461(l) excess-loss cap not modeled.
8

Augusta Rule

Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §280A(g).
MethodRent your home to your business ≤14 days/yr at documented FMV — business deducts it, you exclude it.
Per yearOffset = days × FMV, capped (e.g. 14 × $1,500 = $21,000) → ~$8,500 saved. Fixed, small.
Applies: client has an S-corp/partnership and a bona fide business use (board meetings, etc.).
Skip: no entity or no legitimate business purpose — IRS scrutinizes the FMV and substance.
9

401(k) + Profit Sharing

Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §401(k), §415(c) ($72K addition + catch-up), §404.
MethodDefer up to $72K (+$8K age 50, +$11,250 age 60–63) into the plan.
Per dollar deferred$1 offset → ~40¢ saved — but it's deferral, taxed on withdrawal.
Applies: client has earned income (W-2/SE) and a plan. Recurs annually.
Skip: no earned income (the sale gain doesn't count) or already maxed.
10

Aircraft Purchase

Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §168(k) bonus; §280F(d)(6) qualified business use; §274 entertainment limits.
Method100% bonus depreciation in year 1 when qualified business use exceeds 50%.
Per dollar of cost~$1 offset (× QBU%) if >50%; otherwise ADS over 6 yrs (~17¢).
Applies: >50% qualified business use and the client wants the plane anyway.
Skip: ≤50% QBU, heavy personal/entertainment use (§274), or deduction exceeds the ordinary pool (engine caps it).
11

Short-Term Rental

Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §469 + Reg. §1.469-1T(e)(3)(ii)(A) (the ≤7-day STR exception); §168(k).
Method≤7-day average stays + material participation → non-passive; cost-seg accelerates ~30% of basis.
Per dollar of property basis~30¢ offset year 1 → ~12¢ saved.
Applies: average guest stay ≤7 days AND material participation.
Skip: either fails — the loss becomes passive and suspends (engine returns $0 net).
12

Farm / Business Equipment

Ordinary
AuthorityIRC §179 ($2.56M cap, phase-out begins $4.09M) + §168(k) bonus.
Method§179 immediate expensing + 100% bonus depreciation on the residual.
Per dollar of equipment~$1 offset year 1 (subject to the business-income limit).
Applies: an operating business/farm buys equipment and has business income to absorb §179.
Skip: no business income (§179 carries forward) or spend > $4.09M (dollar-for-dollar phase-out).
